Courses taught

As a rule, every academic year I teach HIS 17B, the introductory U.S. survey from the Civil War to the present, in addition to a selection of upper-level undergraduate courses including HIS 119 (World War I), HIS 120 (World War II), HIS 174B (The United States 1917–1945), HIS 187 (U.S. Foreign Relations in the Twentieth Century), and HIS 188 (The U.S. in the 1960s), as well as graduate seminars in similar subjects. HIS 17B, 119, 120, 174B, 187, and 188 have reliably attracted quite large enrollments for history courses, in the range of 200+ students for 120 and 100–200 for the others.
